    Safe, Sovereign, Expression-Freeing AI in Brazil

    A regulatory-and-technical intervention plan — with a working existence proof

    Global South AI Safety Hackathon 2026 · Latin America track · Governance (with technical + tool cross-cut) Team: ArteLonga · Artifact: the CO platform (AGPL-3.0) + this living analysis.

    Abstract

    AI adoption in Brazil is accelerating inside a partial regulatory vacuum: the LGPD (2018) governs personal data but predates AI, and the AI bill (PL 2338/2023) is still in Congress. Meanwhile, facial recognition, automated decisions, and generative AI trained on Brazilians’ data already produce documented harms — racialized surveillance, algorithmic discrimination, and non-consented data scraping. We (a) map the landscape from primary sources (regulation, harms, and the safe-AI technical toolbox), and (b) propose a prioritized intervention plan anchored in one principle: bounded, local, by-design, open. Crucially, we offer an existence proof — the CO platform already ships these properties — and a roadmap for community-governed Portuguese / Guaraní-Mbyá / Yorùbá models that amplify under-served expression instead of extracting it.

    1. Why Brazil / the Global South

    AI-safety research is concentrated in a few countries; harms in the Global South go under-measured. Two quantified facts frame the urgency: LLMs show up to +195% more safety failures in non-English than in English (arXiv 2504.03174), and a 90.5% racial-bias figure in facial-recognition arrests (CESeC, 2019; of 191 arrested, 42 identifiable, 90.5% Black). Brazil is a high-stakes, under-studied case — exactly this hackathon’s mandate.

    2. Method

    Review of primary sources (statute text, ANPD, Senate/Câmara, TSE, CFM, NIST, OWASP, OSI) and secondary (Brazilian academia + civil society: Data Privacy Brasil, Coding Rights, IDEC, LAPIN, CESeC, ITS Rio, IPEA). Synthesis assisted by parallel research agents with cross-checked citations and adversarial self-review; one unverifiable cost claim was refuted and dropped. Time-sensitive facts are flagged.

    3. Findings — the landscape

    Regulation. No horizontal AI statute in force. PL 2338/2023 (risk-based, EU-AI-Act-inspired) passed the Senate (Dec 2024) and awaits the rapporteur’s opinion in the Câmara (as of Jun 2026) — not enacted; a Dec-2025 government bill (SIA governance) is being appended. LGPD Art. 20 gives only a right to request review of automated decisions — the “by a natural person” requirement was vetoed in 2019, so an algorithm may review an algorithm (weaker than GDPR Art. 22). ANPD is increasingly active: it suspended Meta’s use of Brazilians’ data for genAI training (Jul 2024, R$50k/day), runs a biometrics rulemaking and an AI sandbox (to Dec 2026). Sectoral: CFM 2.454/2026 (human supervision mandatory in medical AI), TSE 23.732/2024 (electoral deepfakes banned).

    Documented harms. Smart Sampa (~50k cameras); wrongful detentions from false matches; 170 Brazilian children’s photos found in LAION-5B (HRW 2024), fueling deepfake abuse; megaleaks feeding profiling; opaque credit/gig decisions; a pt-BR model/eval gap. Meta-finding: the harm narrative outpaces the adjudicated record — outside facial recognition, Brazilian harms are under-measured (reports lean on US cases).

    Technical toolbox. Differential privacy is the connective tissue (federated learning and synthetic data leak without it). Bounded/local inference keeps data on the user’s hardware. Prompt injection is unsolved → containment, not prevention. C2PA signing beats fragile text watermarks. Open-weights (OSAID) buy auditability/sovereignty. Adopt NIST AI RMF + OWASP LLM Top 10 + MITRE ATLAS (free), mapped to LGPD Art. 20; the ANPD sandbox reveals de-facto expectations (transparency/explainability) before PL 2338 passes. Recurring gap: no pt-BR technical/ε standard — also the clearest local value-add.

    4. Intervention — prioritized

    • Technical: open-source a pt-BR PII redaction layer (CPF/CNPJ/RG — absent from Presidio); local-first inference for sensitive data; C2PA provenance; pt-BR safety evals + red-teaming; injection containment + allowlist-on-serve; DP-by-default + cross-silo FL for SUS data.
    • Organizational: adopt the free frameworks now; privacy-by-design baseline (minimal PII, hash secrets, first-party telemetry, no third-party trackers); honest threat model + red-team catalogue; meaningful human review (exceed Art. 20).
    • Public policy: restore meaningful human review (fix the Art. 20 gap via PL 2338’s affected-person rights); strong safeguards/moratorium on public-security facial recognition (the 90.5% bias); ANPD to publish technical guidance (ε/DP); fund auditable pt-BR open models with child-data protection.

    5. Existence proof — we ship it, we don’t just preach it

    The CO platform (AGPL-3.0) already implements the recommendations: LGPD-by-design (no mandatory email; passwords hashed; IPs SHA-256 + daily salt; first-party telemetry; zero third-party trackers), bounded/local intelligence (on-device open weights), defender-advantage scanning in CI (free default + opt-in AI backend; findings are ordinary backlog), an encrypted+signed .co envelope (ChaCha20-Poly1305 + Ed25519), allowlist-on-serve, and an honest AS-IS→TO-BE threat model with a red-team catalogue (including server seizure — a real threat for at-risk communities). The credibility comes from naming what we don’t yet do.

    6. Roadmap — models that free expression

    Evaluate/incorporate local pt-BR models (Maritaca Sabiá (current Sabiá 4; Sabiá-3 benchmark), ~frontier pt-BR, BRL-priced; open TeenyTinyLlama/Gervásio); then train a small, local, open model fine-tuned — only under community governance (CARE/FPIC) — on Portuguese plus the Guaraní-Mbyá Arandu lexicon (~4,600 lemmas, unconfirmed — verify with SIL/community) and Yorùbá terms, with comunicação’s cross-language meaning-topology. Serve it local-first, open-weights, signed, contained — feeding creation tools (walkable lexicon worlds, editors), never automated decisions over people. This is the mirror of the extractive pattern in §3: AI that amplifies ignored languages and creativity.

    7. Implications & limitations

    PL 2338 is a moving target (re-verify before relying). Strong privacy guarantees in CO are partly roadmap (E2E chat, zero-knowledge tier) — labeled, not overclaimed. Indigenous/Afro-language model work is conditional on community consent and benefit — the language belongs to the community, not to us to presume. Several flagship harm reports lean on foreign cases; measuring Brazilian harms is itself a research priority.
