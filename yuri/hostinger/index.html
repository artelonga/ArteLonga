Case study — Retro Umarizal: menu → live Horizons domain

A cold lead with a single restaurant menu became a paying Hostinger customer in ~24h — from a shareable draft to a domain live on Hostinger Horizons.

Jun 2 — arrive
Retro Umarizal arrives with one asset: a restaurant menu. A new lead enters the network.
Jun 2 — instant MVP
A self-hosted staging MVP goes live on a shareable subdomain — no purchase, no DNS for the client to touch. The owner sees a working site within the hour.
Jun 2 — validated
Client approves on the staging URL → buys retroumarizal.com.br. Investment follows validation, not before.
Jun 3 — converted to Horizons
Rebuilt on Hostinger Horizons; the customer compared both and chose the Horizons build. Live on the apex with a Let's Encrypt cert; the self-hosted MVP retired.

A user journey worth measuring

From first contact to a site live on its own domain, the path a customer travels reads as a funnel — a simple, shared way to see where Marketing Impact, activation and retention actually happen, with a number on every step and a department to answer for each (Marketing owns the open; Product the signup; Finance the payment; Infra the go-live; and so on). I modeled it that way, with data-integrity rails (observed-vs-pending, Wilson CIs); the full method — and the stage-by-team breakdown — is in the study.

Customer feedback on Horizons — what I'd report as the analyst

First-hand signal from building retroumarizal.com.br on Horizons — the honest product feedback this role turns into roadmap.

👍 Worked well
Quick intake, feedback incorporated effectively — credit-cheap to a working prototype. Approved within 24h, final edits on free credits. The completion sound is genuinely satisfying. Far easier than deploying and tuning DNS by hand.
🔧 Friction
Initial chat hides the start of the conversation at 100% zoom (recording ↗); some early instructions skipped (e.g. "scrape this site for content"); content needed structured HTML — a URL or pasted copy didn't take (3 exchanges, credit-heavy for content); unclear how to edit after publishing; English by default.
📊 Analyst ask
Surface build telemetry from the start — credits per stage, exchanges-to-prototype, edit-after-publish rate: the unit economics the Horizons dashboard needs.
