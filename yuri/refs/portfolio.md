Two parallel tracks tell this story above — switch between them and step through. One is **mine**; the other is **ours**.

**Data — my path.** A decade of working with data as it grew: field and lab collection in research, then HIPAA-grade storage and management of human-subjects data, three published papers and a patent, retail analytics at scale, and quant. That arc is personal — it lives on [github.com/yurisugano](https://github.com/yurisugano).

**Systems / Web — our network, ArteLonga.** ArteLonga is a collective: a network for career, brand, technology and communication. Here **we** build **co** — one identity, one bill, free software (GPL) we hand to whoever arrives, from a quilombo to a burger shop. Every surface we make (the rfq market-making intelligence, the yggdrasil game engine, the language and neuroscience universes) runs on it. We take a new partner live in minutes as a self-hosted subdomain, validate, then graduate them to their own domain on Hostinger — so the network scales horizontally, at ever-lower cost. We already graduated Retro Umarizal; many more are on the way. Our code lives at [github.com/artelonga](https://github.com/artelonga).

What the network is *for*: putting privacy-respecting software and a real web presence within reach of the small businesses and communities that big platforms price out.
